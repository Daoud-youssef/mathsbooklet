Essential Operation Rules

Simplifying Fractions

💡 Pro Tip A fraction is fully simplified when GCF = 1

How to Simplify

  1. Find the Greatest Common Factor (GCF) of numerator and denominator
  2. Divide both top and bottom by the GCF

\[\frac{a}{b} = \frac{a \div \text{GCF}}{b \div \text{GCF}}\]

Examples: - \(\frac{12}{18} = \frac{12 \div 6}{18 \div 6} = \frac{2}{3}\) - \(\frac{15}{25} = \frac{15 \div 5}{25 \div 5} = \frac{3}{5}\)

Rules of Signs (Multiplication & Division)

🎯 Memory Trick Same signs = Happy (+)
Different signs = Sad (-)

Same Signs → Positive - \((+) \times (+) = (+)\) - \((-) \times (-) = (+)\)

Different Signs → Negative - \((+) \times (-) = (-)\) - \((-) \times (+) = (-)\)

Adding/Subtracting Fractions

⚠️ Common Mistake Don’t add denominators!
WRONG: \(\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{3} = \frac{2}{5}\)

Same Denominator: Keep denominator, add/subtract numerators \[\frac{a}{c} + \frac{b}{c} = \frac{a + b}{c}\]

Multiplying & Dividing Fractions

📝 Quick Method Cancel BEFORE you multiply - saves time!

Multiply: \(\frac{a}{b} \times \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a \times c}{b \times d}\)

Divide (Keep-Change-Flip): \(\frac{a}{b} \div \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a}{b} \times \frac{d}{c}\)

Order of Operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS)

  1. Parentheses / Brackets
  2. Exponents / Orders
  3. M/D Multiplication & Division (Left to Right)
  4. A/S Addition & Subtraction (Left to Right)

ImportantLevel 2: Applied Context

Calculate:
a) Zaid earns 800 QAR a month. He spends \(\frac{1}{4}\) on rent and \(\frac{2}{5}\) on food. How much money does he have left?
b) Fatima is buying shoes that normally cost 200 QAR. They are \(\frac{1}{5}\) off. What is the sale price?
c) Amir has 300 marbles. He gives \(\frac{1}{2}\) to his brother and \(\frac{1}{3}\) of the remaining marbles to his friend. How many does he keep?

Tip🔍 Solutions

  1. Rent: \((800 \div 4) \times 1 = 200\)
    Food: \((800 \div 5) \times 2 = 320\)
    Total spent: \(200 + 320 = 520\)
    Left: \(800 - 520 = 280\) QAR

  2. Discount: \((200 \div 5) \times 1 = 40\)
    Sale Price: \(200 - 40 = 160\) QAR

  3. To brother: \(300 \times \frac{1}{2} = 150\)
    Remaining: \(150\)
    To friend: \(150 \times \frac{1}{3} = 50\)
    Kept: \(150 - 50 = 100\)

Exercise 1.4 (Savings and Values) For every £200 Mrs. Joy earns, she saves £34.

  1. Work out £34 as a percentage of £200.
  2. Last month she earned £1000. How much does she save?
Tip🔍 View Solution

  2. \(17\%\) of \(£1000 = 0.17 \times 1000 = \mathbf{£170}\)

ImportantMulti-Step Reasoning

  1. Maryam works 8 hours a week and earns 50 QAR per hour. She decides to save \(\frac{2}{5}\) of her total weekly earnings. How many weeks will it take her to save 640 QAR?

  2. There are 40 students in a class. \(\frac{3}{5}\) are girls. \(\frac{1}{4}\) of the girls wear glasses. How many girls in the class wear glasses?

Tip🔍 Solutions

  1. Weekly Pay: \(8 \times 50 = 400\)
    Weekly Savings: \((400 \div 5) \times 2 = 160\)
    Weeks needed: \(640 \div 160 = 4\) weeks

  2. Total Girls: \((40 \div 5) \times 3 = 24\)
    Girls with glasses: \((24 \div 4) \times 1 = 6\)
