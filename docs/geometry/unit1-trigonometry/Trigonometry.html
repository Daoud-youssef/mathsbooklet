8.2 🗂️ Formula Card

Trigonometry — Right-Angled Triangles

Labelling the Triangle

💡 Key Rule The labels opposite, adjacent, and hypotenuse depend on which angle θ you are working from. The hypotenuse is always opposite the right angle — it never changes.

Three sides relative to angle θ

  • Hypotenuse (H) — longest side, always opposite the right angle
  • Opposite (O) — the side directly across from angle θ
  • Adjacent (A) — the side next to θ (not the hypotenuse)

SOH-CAH-TOA

🎯 Memory Trick — Some Old Houses Can Always Hide Their Old Age

The three ratios

\[\sin\theta = \frac{O}{H} \qquad \cos\theta = \frac{A}{H} \qquad \tan\theta = \frac{O}{A}\]

Choosing the right ratio — identify which two sides are involved (known + unknown), then pick the ratio that contains both of them.

Known & Unknown Ratio to use
Opp and Hyp sin
Adj and Hyp cos
Opp and Adj tan

Finding a Missing Side

⚠️ Common Mistake Check whether the unknown is on the top or bottom before rearranging. \(x = H \times \sin\theta\) is very different from \(x = \dfrac{O}{\sin\theta}\).

Unknown on top — multiply

\[\sin\theta = \frac{x}{H} \implies x = H \times \sin\theta\]

\[\cos\theta = \frac{x}{H} \implies x = H \times \cos\theta\]

\[\tan\theta = \frac{x}{A} \implies x = A \times \tan\theta\]

Unknown on bottom — divide

\[\sin\theta = \frac{O}{x} \implies x = \frac{O}{\sin\theta}\]

\[\cos\theta = \frac{A}{x} \implies x = \frac{A}{\cos\theta}\]

\[\tan\theta = \frac{O}{x} \implies x = \frac{O}{\tan\theta}\]

Example — find \(x\) given hypotenuse = 10 cm, θ = 30°: \[\sin 30° = \frac{x}{10} \implies x = 10 \times \sin 30° = 5 \text{ cm}\]

Finding a Missing Angle

📝 Key Idea Use the inverse trig function to undo the ratio. On your calculator: press SHIFT (or 2nd) then sin / cos / tan.

The three inverse functions

\[\theta = \sin^{-1}\!\left(\frac{O}{H}\right)\]

\[\theta = \cos^{-1}\!\left(\frac{A}{H}\right)\]

\[\theta = \tan^{-1}\!\left(\frac{O}{A}\right)\]

Example — find θ given opposite = 5 cm, hypotenuse = 13 cm: \[\sin\theta = \frac{5}{13} \implies \theta = \sin^{-1}\!\left(\frac{5}{13}\right) \approx 22.6°\]

Angles of Elevation & Depression

💡 Key Fact Both angles are measured from the horizontal. They are equal (alternate angles) when the ground is level.

Elevation — angle measured upward from horizontal

\[\tan\theta = \frac{h}{d} \qquad h = d\tan\theta \qquad d = \frac{h}{\tan\theta}\]

Depression — angle measured downward from horizontal

\[\tan\theta = \frac{h}{d} \qquad \text{(same formula — alternate angles)}\]

Angle Sum Reminder

In any right-angled triangle the two acute angles sum to 90°: \[\alpha + \beta = 90° \implies \beta = 90° - \alpha\]
